Upper Division Workshop
 

Curious about what upper division math classes are like? Need help planning out which math courses to take next academic year? Either way, this workshop is for you! The Upper Division Workshop will introduce you to the upper-division math topics offered at UCSD and you can ask questions and bring up concerns. The workshop will covers main topics in undergraduate upper-div mathematics with classic examples. Topics will include:

1. Real Analysis (140/142 series)
2. Modern Algebra (100/103 series)
3. Numerical Analysis (170/171 series)
4. Probability (180 series)
5. Statistics (181 series)
6. Combinatorics (184)
7. Graph Theory (154/158)
8. Complex Analysis (120 series)

We are also going to talk about how to choose between classes and get academically ready for graduate school.

Freshman/Transfer Information Workshop
(All-inclusive; not limited to Mathematics Department Freshman/Transfers)

In this workshop we will be introducing you to the lower division requirements for math majors, including the difference between the 10, 20, and 31 Series for Calculus, resources for these classes (tutoring, office hours, MATLAB help, etc.)  and general resources that the Math Department and UCSD can provide for you during your time here. Depending on the remaining time, we may also cover the major upper division series.
